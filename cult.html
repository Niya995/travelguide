List of Cultural Spots in Kerala

  • Kochi-Muziris Biennale

    The Kochi-Muziris Biennale is an international art exhibition held in Kochi, showcasing contemporary art from across the world. It’s an iconic cultural event in Kerala.

  • Kalari Payattu Centre

    Located in Kochi, Kalari Payattu is a traditional martial art form from Kerala. Visitors can witness demonstrations and learn about the cultural significance of this ancient practice.

  • Sree Padmanabhaswamy Temple

    This historic temple in Thiruvananthapuram is a significant cultural and spiritual landmark, known for its Dravidian style architecture and cultural heritage.

  • Chidambaram Temple

    Situated in Kottarakkara, this temple is an important cultural and religious site in Kerala, offering a glimpse into the state's rich temple architecture and rituals.

  • Vasco da Gama Square

    This square in Fort Kochi is named after the Portuguese explorer Vasco da Gama and is a symbol of Kerala's colonial history and cultural connections with the West.

  • Thiruvathira Dance

    Thiruvathira is a traditional Kerala dance performed during the Onam festival. It holds immense cultural significance and offers a vivid portrayal of Kerala’s cultural heritage.

  • Kuthira Malika

    Located in Thiruvananthapuram, Kuthira Malika is a historical palace that showcases Kerala's royal history and culture, with beautiful architecture and intricate woodwork.

  • Theyyam Performance

    Theyyam is a traditional ritualistic dance form in northern Kerala. It’s a vibrant and unique cultural performance held in temples and public spaces, representing the divine and folk traditions.

  • Kerala Folk Art Museum

    Located in Kochi, this museum preserves Kerala’s rich folk traditions, including traditional art forms, dance, and music, offering an in-depth view of the state’s cultural heritage.

  • Raviz Kumarakom

    This cultural spot along the Vembanadu Lake in Kottayam is a serene retreat that offers a chance to experience Kerala’s culture, especially through Kathakali performances and local music.
