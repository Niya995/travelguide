List of Mountains in Kerala

  • Anamudi Peak

    Located in Idukki district, Anamudi is the highest peak in Kerala, standing at 2,695 meters above sea level. It's part of the Western Ghats and offers beautiful views and trekking opportunities.

  • Agasthyakoodam

    Agasthyakoodam is the second highest peak in Kerala, located in the Agasthyakoodam Biosphere Reserve in Idukki district, with an altitude of 1,868 meters. It's famous for its rich biodiversity.

  • Meesapulimala

    Meesapulimala, situated in the Western Ghats, is the second highest peak in the Idukki district at an altitude of 2,640 meters. It's known for its scenic trekking trails and views.

  • Muniyara

    Muniyara, located in the Idukki district, is a historical site and one of the prominent hills in Kerala, offering an ancient atmosphere with megalithic structures.

  • Ponmudi

    Ponmudi is a hill station located in the Thiruvananthapuram district, standing at 1,100 meters above sea level. It offers a beautiful environment for trekking and nature walks.

  • Vavar Peak

    Vavar Peak is located in the Idukki district, part of the Western Ghats, and is known for its lush greenery, peaceful environment, and the famous pilgrimage trail.

  • Ilaveezhapoonchira

    Situated in the Kottayam district, Ilaveezhapoonchira is a scenic hilltop offering panoramic views, trekking opportunities, and a serene environment.

  • Rajamalai

    Rajamalai, located in the Eravikulam National Park, is a popular trekking spot in Idukki, known for the endangered Nilgiri Tahr and breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.

  • Chilappathiripara

    Chilappathiripara is a beautiful mountain located in the Idukki district, known for its pleasant climate, scenic beauty, and ideal trekking opportunities.

  • Chembra Peak

    Chembra Peak, located in Wayanad, stands at 2,100 meters and is famous for its heart-shaped lake, along with its adventurous trekking routes.
