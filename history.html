List of Historical Places in Kerala

  • Padmanabhapuram Palace

    Located in Kanyakumari district, this historical palace was once the seat of the rulers of Travancore. It is known for its beautiful wooden architecture and ancient murals.

  • Fort Kochi

    Fort Kochi, part of Kochi city, is famous for its colonial-era structures, including the St. Francis Church, Chinese fishing nets, and Dutch Cemetery.

  • Bekal Fort

    Located in Kasaragod district, Bekal Fort is a historic fort that offers stunning views of the Arabian Sea, with its large observation tower and scenic surroundings.

  • Thrissur Zoo and Museum

    Founded in 1857, the Thrissur Zoo is home to a variety of animals and also houses a museum showcasing Kerala's rich cultural heritage and natural history.

  • Mattancherry Palace

    Also known as the Dutch Palace, located in Kochi, it showcases the history of the region with paintings and murals that reflect the royal life of the Kochi dynasty.

  • Alappuzha Pier

    Alappuzha Pier is a historical landmark in the Alappuzha district, known for its importance in Kerala's maritime history and as a prominent hub for the spice trade.

  • Jew Street

    Located in Fort Kochi, Jew Street is famous for its antique shops, synagogues, and heritage structures, reflecting the cultural interaction between Jews and local communities.

  • Valliyoorkavu Temple

    Valliyoorkavu Temple in Kottayam district is an ancient shrine dedicated to the goddess Bhagavathi, showcasing Kerala's unique temple architecture and cultural practices.

  • Dutch Cemetery

    Located in Fort Kochi, the Dutch Cemetery is a reminder of the colonial past, where several Dutch and Portuguese officials are buried, reflecting the region's European heritage.

  • Malik Ibn Dinar Mosque

    Situated in Kozhikode, this mosque is one of the oldest in Kerala, with historical significance related to the early spread of Islam in the region.
